# Takeaways/My thoughts
- Don't focus too much on technical mastery because advancements in technology will eventually lessen its value instead,  focus on being good at knowing what x business is suffering from and then coming up with a solution for it. (In this video's case, communicate that problem to an AI so that they'll come up with a solution for it.)
	- What not to focus: technical mastery (tool usage), processes, workflows, etc.
	- What to focus: Understanding business systems & patterns in value creation 
- Technical skills is not everything, it's just a part of what makes a system work. It's required 100%. But, it's important not to get lost in the sauce I guess, is one way to put it. 
- It's super important to be able to diagnose business problems and provide solutions.
	- Now I'll try diagnosing what problems my business is suffering from:
		- The biggest problem is - > ME <- , I burn out, I get distracted, I don't feel motivated, I get lazy, I get depressed, etc. etc.
		- Say current business: 
				- Customer service (Accommodating commission customers): ME
				- Fulfillment (Drawing the art): ME
			- Just from this alone, I can already say, customer service and fulfillment is bound to fail. Which means the business is bound to fail.
			- So maybe, I need to switch to a different business. I mean, I can try outsourcing these but what's the point then? I would stop being an artist if I do that.
		- Say I change business:
				- Business is T-Shirt Printing
				- Designing T-Shirt: ME
				- Customer service/Shopfront: idk, some platform that wants to sell stuff (Fourthwall)
				- Printing: some platform that wants to print designs (Fourthwall)
				- Shipping/Logistics: idk, some platform that wants to ship stuff (Fourthwall)
				- Marketing: Either ME or I outsource it/find other methods.
			- With this, if ME breaks/get lazy, assuming I already made designs:
				- Business will still run, only that there's no new designs instead of it just outright DYING
